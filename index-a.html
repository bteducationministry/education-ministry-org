Civic Knowledge Was Never Meant
to Be This Rare

Throughout history, the strength of a society has been measured by the civic virtue of its people—their understanding of rights, duties, and governance. Today, that foundation is crumbling.

To Know God
Is to Know Thyself

And to know the difference between your public duty and your private standing.

The families who build lasting legacies don't do it through accumulation—they do it through knowledge and posture.

They understand covenant. They understand stewardship. They understand the distinction between their standing as living heirs in the private and their civic duties in the public.

This knowledge isn't hidden—it's in plain view. Estates and trusts. Constitutional principles. Executor and trustee skills. The operating system God established for families to build, protect, and preserve across generations.

But modern society has replaced this foundation with commercial traps and social norms that keep families operating entirely in the public sphere—unaware that a private posture even exists.

BT Education Ministry exists to restore this knowledge. Faith-grounded civic education that teaches you to discern private from public, to steward what God has entrusted to you, and to fulfill your civic duties without surrendering your standing.

The Young Civic Engagement Challenge

Step forward. Do your part. Learn what every family should know.

Every functioning society requires its people to fulfill a bare minimum of civic duties. Most never do—because no one taught them what those duties are.

The Young Civic Engagement Challenge is a free, structured introduction to civic virtue. It challenges you to step forward, understand your role in your community, and take the first practical steps toward civic engagement.

Using the principle of “As Above, So Below,” you'll discover that the same civic virtues required in public life—accountability, stewardship, governance—are first practiced and secured in your private household.
